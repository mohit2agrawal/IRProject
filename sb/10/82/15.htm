Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 82: Kṛṣṇa and Balarāma Meet the Inhabitants of Vṛndāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.82.15

striyaś ca saḿvīkṣya mitho 'ti-sauhṛda-

smitāmalāpāńga-dṛśo 'bhirebhire

stanaiḥ stanān kuńkuma-pańka-rūṣitān

nihatya dorbhiḥ praṇayāśru-locanāḥ

SYNONYMS

striyaḥ — the women; ca — and; saḿvīkṣya — seeing; mithaḥ — one another; ati — extreme; sauhṛda — with friendly affection; smita — smiling; amala — pure; apāńga — exhibiting glances; dṛśaḥ — whose eyes; abhirebhire — they embraced; stanaiḥ — with breasts; stanān — breasts; kuńkuma — of saffron; pańka — with paste; rūṣitān — smeared; nihatya — pressing; dorbhiḥ — with their arms; praṇaya — of love; aśru — tears; locanāḥ — in whose eyes.

TRANSLATION

The women glanced at one another with pure smiles of loving friendship. And when they embraced, their breasts, smeared with saffron paste, pressed against one another as their eyes filled with tears of affection.
