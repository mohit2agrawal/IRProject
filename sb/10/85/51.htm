Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Kṛṣṇa Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.51

smarodgīthaḥ pariṣvańgaḥ

patańgaḥ kṣudrabhṛd ghṛṇī

ṣaḍ ime mat-prasādena

punar yāsyanti sad-gatim

SYNONYMS

smara-udgīthaḥ pariṣvańgaḥ — Smara, Udgītha and Pariṣvańga; patańgaḥ kṣudrabhṛt ghṛṇī — Patańga, Kṣudrabhṛt and Ghṛṇī; ṣaṭ — six; ime — these; mat — My; prasādena — by the grace; punaḥ — again; yāsyanti — will go; sat — of saintly persons; gatim — to the destination.

TRANSLATION

By My grace these six — Smara, Udgītha, Pariṣvańga, Patańga, Kṣudrabhṛt and Ghṛṇī — will return to the abode of pure saints.

PURPORT

These are the names the six children first had when they were sons of Marīci. The oldest, Smara, was called Kīrtimān when born again to Vasudeva, as recorded in the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.1.57),

kīrtimantaḿ prathama-jaḿ

kaḿsāyānakadundubhiḥ

arpayām āsa kṛcchreṇa

so 'nṛtad ati-vihvalaḥ

"Vasudeva was very much disturbed by the fear of becoming a liar by breaking his promise. Thus with great pain he delivered his firstborn son, named Kīrtimān, into the hands of Kaḿsa."
