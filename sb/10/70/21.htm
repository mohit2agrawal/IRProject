Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 70: Lord Kṛṣṇa's Daily Activities

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.70.21

tatrāhur brāhmaṇāḥ kecid

āsīnā brahma-vādinaḥ

pūrveṣāḿ puṇya-yaśasāḿ

rājñāḿ cākathayan kathāḥ

SYNONYMS

tatra — there; āhuḥ — spoke; brāhmaṇāḥ — brāhmaṇas; kecit — some; āsīnāḥ — seated; brahma — in the Vedas; vādinaḥ — fluent; pūrveṣām — of those of the past; puṇya — pious; yaśasām — whose fame; rājñām — of kings; ca — and; ākathayan — they recounted; kathāḥ — stories.

TRANSLATION

Some brāhmaṇas sitting in that assembly hall would fluently chant Vedic mantras, while others recounted stories of past kings of pious renown.
