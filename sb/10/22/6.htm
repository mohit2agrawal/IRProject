Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 22: Kṛṣṇa Steals the Garments of the Unmarried Gopīs

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.22.6

ūṣasy utthāya gotraiḥ svair

anyonyābaddha-bāhavaḥ

kṛṣṇam uccair jagur yāntyaḥ

kālindyāḿ snātum anvaham

SYNONYMS

ūṣasi — at dawn; utthāya — rising; gotraiḥ — by their names; svaiḥ — proper; anyonya — one with another; ābaddha — holding; bāhavaḥ — their hands; kṛṣṇam — in glorification of Kṛṣṇa; uccaiḥ — loudly; jaguḥ — they sang; yāntyaḥ — while going; kālindyām — to the Yamunā; snātum — in order to bathe; anu-aham — each day.

TRANSLATION

Each day they rose at dawn. Calling out to one another by name, they all held hands and loudly sang the glories of Kṛṣṇa while going to the Kālindī to take their bath.
