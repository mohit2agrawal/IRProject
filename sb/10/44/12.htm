Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kaḿsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.12

kiḿ na paśyata rāmasya

mukham ātāmra-locanam

muṣṭikaḿ prati sāmarṣaḿ

hāsa-saḿrambha-śobhitam

SYNONYMS

kim — why; na paśyata — do you not see; rāmasya — of Lord Balarāma; mukham — the face; ātāmra — like copper; locanam — with eyes; muṣṭikam — Muṣṭika; prati — toward; sa-amarṣam — with anger; hāsa — by His laughter; saḿrambha — and His absorption; śobhitam — beautified.

TRANSLATION

Don't you see the face of Lord Balarāma, with its eyes copper-red from His anger toward Muṣṭika and its beauty enhanced by His laughter and His absorption in the fight?
