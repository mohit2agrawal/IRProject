Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 78: The Killing of Dantavakra, Vidūratha and Romaharṣaṇa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.78.9

gadā-nirbhinna-hṛdaya

udvaman rudhiraḿ mukhāt

prasārya keśa-bāhv-ańghrīn

dharaṇyāḿ nyapatad vyasuḥ

SYNONYMS

gadā — by the club; nirbhinna — broken to pieces; hṛdayaḥ — his heart; udvaman — vomiting; rudhiram — blood; mukhāt — from his mouth; prasārya — thrusting outward; keśa — his hair; bāhu — arms; ańghrīn — and legs; dharaṇyām — on the ground; nyapatat — he fell; vyasuḥ — lifeless.

TRANSLATION

His heart shattered by the club's blow, Dantavakra vomited blood and fell lifeless to the ground, his hair disheveled and his arms and legs sprawling.
