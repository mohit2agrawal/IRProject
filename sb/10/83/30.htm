Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 83: Draupadī Meets the Queens of Kṛṣṇa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.83.30

tāvan mṛdańga-paṭahāḥ

śańkha-bhery-ānakādayaḥ

ninedur naṭa-nartakyo

nanṛtur gāyakā jaguḥ

SYNONYMS

tāvat — just then; mṛdańga-paṭahāḥ — mṛdańga and paṭaha drums; śańkha — conchshells; bherī — kettledrums; ānaka — large military drums; ādayaḥ — and so on; nineduḥ — resounded; naṭa — male dancers; nartakyaḥ — and female dancers; nanṛtuḥ — danced; gāyakāḥ — singers; jaguḥ — sang.

TRANSLATION

Just then there were loud sounds of conchshells and mṛdańga, paṭaha, bherī and ānaka drums, as well as other instruments. Men and women began to dance, and singers began to sing.
