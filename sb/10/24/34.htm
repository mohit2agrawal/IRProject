Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 24: Worshiping Govardhana Hill

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.24.34

anāḿsy anaḍud-yuktāni

te cāruhya sv-alańkṛtāḥ

gopyaś ca kṛṣṇa-vīryāṇi

gāyantyaḥ sa-dvijāśiṣaḥ

SYNONYMS

anāḿsi — wagons; anaḍut-yuktāni — yoked with oxen; te — they; ca — and; āruhya — riding; su-alańkṛtāḥ — nicely ornamented; gopyaḥ — the cowherd women; ca — and; kṛṣṇa-vīryāṇi — the glories of Lord Kṛṣṇa; gāyantyaḥ — singing; sa — together with; dvija — of the brāhmaṇas; āśiṣaḥ — the benedictions.

TRANSLATION

As the beautifully ornamented cowherd ladies followed along, riding on wagons drawn by oxen, they sang the glories of Lord Kṛṣṇa, and their songs mingled with the brāhmaṇas' chanting of benedictions.
