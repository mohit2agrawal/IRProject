Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 15: The Killing of Dhenuka, the Ass Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.15.15

nṛtyato gāyataḥ kvāpi

valgato yudhyato mithaḥ

gṛhīta-hastau gopālān

hasantau praśaśaḿsatuḥ

SYNONYMS

nṛtyataḥ — who were dancing; gāyataḥ — singing; kva api — sometimes; valgataḥ — moving about; yudhyataḥ — fighting; mithaḥ — with one another; gṛhīta-hastau — holding Their hands together; gopālān — the cowherd boys; hasantau — laughing; praśaśaḿsatuḥ — They offered praise.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes, as the cowherd boys danced, sang, moved about and playfully fought with each other, Kṛṣṇa and Balarāma, standing nearby hand in hand, would glorify Their friends' activities and laugh.
