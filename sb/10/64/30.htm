Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 64: The Deliverance of King Nṛga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.64.30

ity uktvā taḿ parikramya

pādau spṛṣṭvā sva-maulinā

anujñāto vimānāgryam

āruhat paśyatāḿ nṛṇām

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; uktvā — having spoken; tam — Him; parikramya — circumambulating; pādau — His feet; spṛṣṭvā — touching; sva — with his; maulinā — crown; anujñātaḥ — given leave; vimāna — a celestial airplane; agryam — excellent; āruhat — he boarded; paśyatām — as they watched; nṛṇām — humans.

TRANSLATION

Having spoken thus, Mahārāja Nṛga circumambulated Lord Kṛṣṇa and touched his crown to the Lord's feet. Granted permission to depart, King Nṛga then boarded a wonderful celestial airplane as all the people present looked on.
