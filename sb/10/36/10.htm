Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.10

agra-nyasta-viṣāṇāgraḥ

stabdhāsṛg-locano 'cyutam

kaṭākṣipyādravat tūrṇam

indra-mukto 'śanir yathā

SYNONYMS

agra — forward; nyasta — pointing; viṣāṇa — of his horns; agraḥ — the front; stabdha — glaring; asṛk — bloody; locanaḥ — his eyes; acyutam — at Lord Kṛṣṇa; kaṭa-ākṣipya — looking sideways; adravat — he ran; tūrṇam — at full speed; indra-muktaḥ — released by King Indra; aśaniḥ — a thunderbolt; yathā — like.

TRANSLATION

Pointing the tips of his horns straight ahead and glaring menacingly at Lord Kṛṣṇa from the corners of his bloodshot eyes, Ariṣṭa rushed toward Him at full speed, like a thunderbolt hurled by Indra.
