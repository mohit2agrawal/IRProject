Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 63: Lord Kṛṣṇa Fights with Bāṇāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.63.23

atha nārāyaṇaḥ devaḥ

taḿ dṛṣṭvā vyasṛjaj jvaram

māheśvaro vaiṣṇavaś ca

yuyudhāte jvarāv ubhau

SYNONYMS

atha — thereupon; nārāyaṇaḥ devaḥ — Lord Nārāyaṇa (Kṛṣṇa); tam — him (the Śiva-jvara); dṛṣṭvā — seeing; vyasṛjat — released; jvaram — His personified fever (of extreme cold, as opposed to the extreme heat of the Śiva-jvara); māheśvaraḥ — of Lord Māheśvara; vaiṣṇavaḥ — of Lord Viṣṇu; ca — and; yuyudhāte — fought; jvarau — the two fevers; ubhau — against each other.

TRANSLATION

Seeing this personified weapon approach, Lord Nārāyaṇa then released His own personified fever weapon, the Viṣṇu-jvara. The Śiva-jvara and Viṣṇu-jvara thus battled each other.
