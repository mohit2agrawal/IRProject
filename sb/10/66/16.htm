Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Pauṇḍraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.16

śulair gadābhiḥ parighaiḥ

śakty-ṛṣṭi-prāsa-tomaraiḥ

asibhiḥ paṭṭiśair bāṇaiḥ

prāharann arayo harim

SYNONYMS

śūlaiḥ — with tridents; gadābhiḥ — clubs; parighaiḥ — and bludgeons; śakti — pikes; ṛṣṭi — a kind of sword; prāsa — long, barbed darts; tomaraiḥ — and lances; asibhiḥ — with swords; paṭṭiśaiḥ — with axes; bāṇaiḥ — and with arrows; prāharan — attacked; arayaḥ — the enemies; harim — Lord Kṛṣṇa.

TRANSLATION

The enemies of Lord Hari attacked Him with tridents, clubs, bludgeons, pikes, ṛṣtis, barbed darts, lances, swords, axes and arrows.
