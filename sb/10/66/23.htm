Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Pauṇḍraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.23

evaḿ matsariṇam hatvā

pauṇḍrakaḿ sa-sakhaḿ hariḥ

dvārakām āviśat siddhair

gīyamāna-kathāmṛtaḥ

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; matsariṇam — envious; hatvā — killing; pauṇḍrakam — Pauṇḍraka; sa — together with; sakham — his friend; hariḥ — Lord Kṛṣṇa; dvārakām — Dvārakā; āviśat — He entered; siddhaiḥ — by the mystics of heaven; gīyamāna — being sung; kathā — narrations about Him; amṛtaḥ — nectarean.

TRANSLATION

Having thus killed envious Pauṇḍraka and his ally, Lord Kṛṣṇa returned to Dvārakā. As He entered the city, the Siddhas of heaven chanted His immortal, nectarean glories.
