Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 21: The Movements of the Sun

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.21.17

tathā vālikhilyā ṛṣayo 'ńguṣṭha-parva-mātrāḥ ṣaṣṭi-sahasrāṇi purataḥ sūryaḿ sūkta-vākāya niyuktāḥ saḿstuvanti

SYNONYMS

tathā — there; vālikhilyāḥ — Vālikhilyas; ṛṣayaḥ — great sages; ańguṣṭha-parva-mātrāḥ — whose size is that of a thumb; ṣaṣṭi-sahasrāṇi — sixty thousand; purataḥ — in front; sūryam — the sun-god; su-ukta-vākāya — for speaking eloquently; niyuktāḥ — engaged; saḿstuvanti — offer prayers.

TRANSLATION

There are sixty thousand saintly persons named Vālikhilyas, each the size of a thumb, who are located in front of the sun-god and who offer him eloquent prayers of glorification.
