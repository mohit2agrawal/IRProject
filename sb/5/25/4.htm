Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 25: The Glories of Lord Ananta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.25.4

yasyāńghri-kamala-yugalāruṇa-viśada-nakha-maṇi-ṣaṇḍa-maṇḍaleṣv ahi-patayaḥ saha sātvatarṣabhair ekānta-bhakti-yogenāvanamantaḥ sva-vadanāni parisphurat-kuṇḍala-prabhā-maṇḍita-gaṇḍa-sthalāny ati-manoharāṇi pramudita-manasaḥ khalu vilokayanti

SYNONYMS

yasya — of whom; ańghri-kamala — of lotus feet; yugala — of the pair; aruṇa-viśada — brilliant pink; nakha — of the nails; maṇi-ṣaṇḍa — like gems; maṇḍaleṣu — on the round surfaces; ahi-patayaḥ — the leaders of the snakes; saha — with; sātvata-ṛṣabhaiḥ — the best devotees; ekānta-bhakti-yogena — with unalloyed devotional service; avanamantaḥ — offering obeisances; sva-vadanāni — their own faces; parisphurat — glittering; kuṇḍala — of the earrings; prabhā — by the effulgence; maṇḍita — decorated; gaṇḍa-sthalāni — whose cheeks; ati-manoharāṇi — very beautiful; pramudita-manasaḥ — their minds refreshed; khalu — indeed; vilokayanti — they see.

TRANSLATION

The pink, transparent toenails on the Lord's lotus feet are exactly like valuable gems polished to a mirror finish. When the unalloyed devotees and the leaders of the snakes offer their obeisances to Lord Sańkarṣaṇa with great devotion, they become very joyful upon seeing their own beautiful faces reflected in His toenails. Their cheeks are decorated with glittering earrings, and the beauty of their faces is extremely pleasing to see.
