Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 28: Jñāna-yoga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.28.39

yoga-dhāraṇayā kāḿścid

āsanair dhāraṇānvitaiḥ

tapo-mantrauṣadhaiḥ kāḿścid

upasargān vinirdahet

SYNONYMS

yoga-dhāraṇayā — by yogic meditation; kāḿścit — some disturbances; āsanaiḥ — by prescribed postures; dhāraṇā-anvitaiḥ — together with meditation on controlled breathing; tapaḥ — by special austerities; mantra — magical chants; auṣadhaiḥ — and medicinal herbs; kāḿścit — some; upasargān — obstructions; vinirdahet — can be eradicated.

TRANSLATION

Some of these obstructions may be counteracted by yogic meditation or by sitting postures, practiced together with concentration on controlled breathing, and others may be counteracted by special austerities, mantras or medicinal herbs.
