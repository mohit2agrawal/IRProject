Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.46

gaccha dvāravatīḿ sūta

jñātīnāḿ nidhanaḿ mithaḥ

sańkarṣaṇasya niryāṇaḿ

bandhubhyo brūhi mad-daśām

SYNONYMS

gaccha — just go; dvāravatīm — to Dvārakā; sūta — O chariot driver; jñātīnām — of their close relatives; nidhanam — the destruction; mithaḥ — mutual; sańkarṣaṇasya — of Lord Balarāma; niryāṇam — the passing away; bandhubhyaḥ — to Our family members; brūhi — speak; mat-daśām — My condition.

TRANSLATION

O driver, go to Dvārakā and tell Our family members how their loved ones destroyed one another. Also tell them of the disappearance of Lord Sańkarṣaṇa and of My present condition.

PURPORT

Lord Kṛṣṇa sent His chariot driverless back to Vaikuṇṭha, along with the horses and weapons, because the chariot driver, Dāruka, had some final service to do on earth.
