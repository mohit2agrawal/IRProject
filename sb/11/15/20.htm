Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 15: Lord Kṛṣṇa's Description of Mystic Yoga Perfections

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.15.20

cakṣus tvaṣṭari saḿyojya

tvaṣṭāram api cakṣuṣi

māḿ tatra manasā dhyāyan

viśvaḿ paśyati dūrataḥ

SYNONYMS

cakṣuḥ — the eyes; tvaṣṭari — in the sun; saḿyojya — merging; tvaṣṭāram — the sun; api — also; cakṣuṣi — in one's eyes; mām — Me; tatra — there, in the mutual merging of sun and eye; manasā — with the mind; dhyāyan — meditating; viśvam — everything; paśyati — he sees; dūrataḥ — far away.

TRANSLATION

Merging one's sight into the sun planet and then the sun planet into one's eyes, one should meditate on Me as existing within the combination of sun and vision; thus one acquires the power to see any distant thing.
