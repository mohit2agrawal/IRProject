Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 21: Instructions by Mahārāja Pṛthu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.21.3

savṛndaiḥ kadalī-stambhaiḥ

pūga-potaiḥ pariṣkṛtam

taru-pallava-mālābhiḥ

sarvataḥ samalańkṛtam

SYNONYMS

sa-vṛndaiḥ — along with fruits and flowers; kadalī-stambhaiḥ — by the pillars of banana trees; pūga-potaiḥ — by collections of young animals and by processions of elephants; pariṣkṛtam — very nicely cleansed; taru — young plants; pallava — new leaves of mango trees; mālābhiḥ — by garlands; sarvataḥ — everywhere; samalańkṛtam — nicely decorated.

TRANSLATION

At the street crossings there were bunches of fruits and flowers, as well as pillars of banana trees and betel nut branches. All these combined decorations everywhere looked very attractive.
