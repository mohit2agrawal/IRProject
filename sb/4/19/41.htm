Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Pṛthu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.41

viprāḥ satyāśiṣas tuṣṭāḥ

śraddhayā labdha-dakṣiṇāḥ

āśiṣo yuyujuḥ kṣattar

ādi-rājāya sat-kṛtāḥ

SYNONYMS

viprāḥ — all the brāhmaṇas; satya — true; āśiṣaḥ — whose benedictions; tuṣṭāḥ — being very satisfied; śraddhayā — with great respect; labdha-dakṣiṇāḥ — who obtained rewards; āśiṣaḥ — benedictions; yuyujuḥ — offered; kṣattaḥ — O Vidura; ādi-rājāya — upon the original king; sat-kṛtāḥ — being honored.

TRANSLATION

With great respect, the original king, Pṛthu, offered all kinds of rewards to the brāhmaṇas present at the sacrifice. Since all these brāhmaṇas were very much satisfied, they gave their heartfelt blessings to the King.
