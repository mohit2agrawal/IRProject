Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 17: Mahārāja Pṛthu Becomes Angry at the Earth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.17.2

brāhmaṇa-pramukhān varṇān

bhṛtyāmātya-purodhasaḥ

paurāñ jāna-padān śreṇīḥ

prakṛtīḥ samapūjayat

SYNONYMS

brāhmaṇa-pramukhān — unto the leaders of the brāhmaṇa community; varṇān — to the other castes; bhṛtya — servants; amātya — ministers; purodhasaḥ — to the priests; paurān — to the citizens; jāna-padān — to his countrymen; śreṇīḥ — to different communities; prakṛtīḥ — to the admirers; samapūjayat — he gave proper respects.

TRANSLATION

King Pṛthu thus satisfied and offered all respect to all the leaders of the brāhmaṇas and other castes, to his servants, to his ministers and to the priests, citizens, general countrymen, people from other communities, admirers and others, and thus they all became happy.
