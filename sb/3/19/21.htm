Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 19: The Killing of the Demon Hiraṇyākṣa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.19.21

bahubhir yakṣa-rakṣobhiḥ

patty-aśva-ratha-kuñjaraiḥ

ātatāyibhir utsṛṣṭā

hiḿsrā vāco 'tivaiśasāḥ

SYNONYMS

bahubhiḥ — by many; yakṣa-rakṣobhiḥ — Yakṣas and Rākṣasas; patti — marching on foot; aśva — on horses; ratha — on chariots; kuñjaraiḥ — or on elephants; ātatāyibhiḥ — ruffians; utsṛṣṭāḥ — were uttered; hiḿsrāḥ — cruel; vācaḥ — words; ati-vaiśasāḥ — murderous.

TRANSLATION

Cruel and savage slogans were uttered by hosts of ruffian Yakṣas and Rākṣasas, who all either marched on foot or rode on horses, elephants or chariots.
