Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 17: Victory of Hiraṇyākṣa Over All the Directions of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.17.7

cukrośa vimanā vārdhir

udūrmiḥ kṣubhitodaraḥ

sodapānāś ca saritaś

cukṣubhuḥ śuṣka-pańkajāḥ

SYNONYMS

cukrośa — wailed aloud; vimanāḥ — stricken with sorrow; vārdhiḥ — the ocean; udūrmiḥ — high waves; kṣubhita — agitated; udaraḥ — the creatures inside; sa-udapānāḥ — with the drinking water of the lakes and the wells; ca — and; saritaḥ — the rivers; cukṣubhuḥ — were agitated; śuṣka — withered; pańkajāḥ — lotus flowers.

TRANSLATION

The ocean with its high waves wailed aloud as if stricken with sorrow, and there was a commotion among the creatures inhabiting the ocean. The rivers and lakes were also agitated, and lotuses withered.
