Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 1: Questions by Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.1.11

ajāta-śatroḥ pratiyaccha dāyaḿ

titikṣato durviṣahaḿ tavāgaḥ

sahānujo yatra vṛkodarāhiḥ

śvasan ruṣā yat tvam alaḿ bibheṣi

SYNONYMS

ajāta-śatroḥ — of Yudhiṣṭhira, who has no enemy; pratiyaccha — return; dāyam — legitimate share; titikṣataḥ — of he who is so forbearing; durviṣaham — unbearable; tava — your; āgaḥ — offense; saha — along with; anujaḥ — younger brothers; yatra — wherein; vṛkodara — Bhīma; ahiḥ — revenging snake; śvasan — breathing heavily; ruṣā — in anger; yat — whom; tvam — you; alam — verily; bibheṣi — do fear.

TRANSLATION

[Vidura said:] You must now return the legitimate share to Yudhiṣṭhira, who has no enemies and who has been forbearing through untold sufferings due to your offenses. He is waiting with his younger brothers, among whom is the revengeful Bhīma, breathing heavily like a snake. Surely you are afraid of him.
