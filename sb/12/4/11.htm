Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 4: The Four Categories of Universal Annihilation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.4.11

tataḥ pracaṇḍa-pavano

varṣāṇām adhikaḿ śatam

paraḥ sāḿvartako vāti

dhūmraḿ khaḿ rajasāvṛtam

SYNONYMS

tataḥ — then; pracaṇḍa — terrible; pavanaḥ — a wind; varṣāṇām — of years; adhikam — more than; śatam — one hundred; paraḥ — great; sāmvartakaḥ — causing annihilation; vāti — blows; dhūmram — gray; kham — the sky; rajasā — with dust; āvṛtam — covered.

TRANSLATION

A great and terrible wind of destruction will begin to blow for more than one hundred years, and the sky, covered with dust, will turn gray.
